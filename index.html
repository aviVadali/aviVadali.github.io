Condensed Matter Physics & Quantum Information

I am a physicist working at the intersection of condensed matter theory and quantum information science. My research explores emergent phenomena in strongly correlated systems, topological phases of matter, and the use of quantum entanglement as a probe of exotic order. I am broadly interested in how information-theoretic ideas illuminate the structure of quantum many-body systems.

Research

My work sits at the intersection of condensed matter theory, quantum information, and mathematical physics. I use a combination of field-theoretic, numerical, and algebraic methods.

⬡

Topological Phases of Matter

I study phases characterized by topological invariants rather than local order parameters. Current projects focus on non-Abelian anyons in fractional quantum Hall systems and their potential as platforms for fault-tolerant quantum computation.

Fractional QH Anyons Topological Order

Quantum Entanglement & Information

Entanglement entropy and quantum information tools provide sharp probes of phase structure in many-body systems. I develop and apply tensor network methods — particularly MERA and DMRG — to characterize entanglement in gapless and topological phases.

Tensor Networks Entanglement Entropy MERA / DMRG
∿

Non-Equilibrium Quantum Dynamics

How do isolated quantum systems thermalize — or fail to? I investigate many-body localization, Floquet phases, and quantum chaos from the perspective of eigenstate thermalization and spectral statistics in disordered spin chains.

MBL Floquet Systems Quantum Chaos
◈

Strongly Correlated Electrons

Strongly interacting electrons give rise to unconventional superconductivity, quantum spin liquids, and heavy-fermion behavior. I use field-theoretic and numerical approaches to understand spin-liquid phases in frustrated magnets and moiré heterostructures.

March 2024 Tensor Networks

A Pedestrian Introduction to MERA

The Multi-scale Entanglement Renormalization Ansatz is a variational class of tensor networks tailored to critical systems. Here I give a self-contained derivation of the causal cone structure and explain why MERA saturates the logarithmic entanglement entropy of CFTs.

November 2023 Topology

Berry Phase, Chern Numbers, and the TKNN Formula

A clean derivation of the TKNN formula connecting the Hall conductance to a first Chern number. Starting from Kubo's formula and using the language of differential geometry on the Brillouin zone torus.

Read note →
August 2023 MBL

Why ETH Fails: An Intuitive Picture of Many-Body Localization

The Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis explains why isolated quantum systems thermalize. MBL is the striking exception. I outline the intuition behind the l-bit picture and the area-law entanglement of MBL eigenstates.
