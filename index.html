Research Work Experience Projects CV

Reinforcement Learning for Fixed-Wing UAV Navigation in Turbulent Environments

Caltech | Prof. Mory Gharib September 2024 – Present

I worked under Professor Mory Gharib to design and build the embedded systems onboard a fixed-wing UAV aircraft. I developed a custom handshake between the Cube Orange Plus flight computer and the Jetson Orin Nano on-board computer to reliably transmit ROS messages in turbulent environments. I also helped design and integrate low-cost pitot tubes capable of measuring wind speed at five distributed locations across the airframe. This data fed into a machine learning model we developed to estimate wind dynamics in real time.

Improving Spacecraft Fault Diagnosis Using Active Sensing and Sensor Failure Modeling

Caltech | Prof. Soon-Jo Chung June – September 2023

I worked under Professor Soon-Jo Chung to build a physics simulation that models spacecraft kinematics and actuator systems as Markov Decision Problems. I expanded the existing 3-DOF spacecraft model to a 6-DOF model, capable of modeling the spacecraft's attitude and positional state. I integrated this physics engine into the lab's existing fault detection, isolation, and recovery algorithms to identify faulty reaction wheels and actuator systems onboard the spacecraft.

Additionally, I developed a cycleGAN model to convert RGB images taken onboard the spacecraft into infrared images. These images were then fed into the lab's existing FDIR algorithms to isolate and recover from various camera failure modes, including hot pixels and solar glare. The final report for this project can be found here.

Machine Learning for Analyte Identification via Raman Spectroscopy

Caltech | Prof. Lu Wei June – September 2022

I worked with Professor Lu Wei to develop a data pipeline for analyzing the Raman spectra of unknown analytes in food samples. I designed a preprocessing and augmentation workflow to generate a robust spectral dataset, which captured the vibrational signatures of a variety of non-homogeneous foods. Using this dataset, I trained and evaluated both k-nearest neighbors (kNN) and random forest classifiers to identify food samples based on their Raman spectra. The resulting models achieved up to 88% classification accuracy.
