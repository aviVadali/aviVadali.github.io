Open questions

Ideas

Questions I keep coming back to. Some are things I am actively working on; others I am simply curious about and cannot stop thinking about. I make no promises about which will become papers.

01

When multiple non-Abelian anyon species proliferate simultaneously in a topologically ordered system, do they independently select distinct confined phases, or does competition between condensation channels give rise to entirely new critical phenomena not predicted by the single-species theory?

Most anyon condensation transitions are analyzed one species at a time. Realistic materials host many anyon types, and their simultaneous proliferation may lead to richer phase diagrams — potentially with novel universality classes or multicritical points. This is the central question of my work with Jason Alicea and Pablo Sala.

topological order non-Abelian anyons phase transitions quantum error correction
02

To what extent do emergent — rather than microscopic — symmetries govern the topology of moiré flat bands, and can one engineer topological critical points by tuning which accidental symmetries are realized near van Hove singularities?

Moiré systems are usually analyzed using the symmetries inherited from constituent lattices. But near band touchings, accidental symmetries can appear that have no microscopic origin. Work with Ashvin Vishwanath and Tomo Soejima suggests these emergent symmetries can completely restructure the Berry curvature and the topology of flat bands.

moiré systems Berry curvature emergent symmetries topological bands
03

Is decoherence in a topological quantum memory a sharp phase transition in the space of mixed states — and if so, what is the right order parameter, and can it be detected without performing full quantum state tomography?

The standard picture is that topological memories decohere gracefully until noise crosses a critical threshold. Framing this as a genuine phase transition in the density matrix raises sharper questions: what universality class governs it, is there a local observable that detects it, and does the transition survive in the presence of correlated noise?

quantum memory decoherence mixed-state phases topological codes
04

Is there a systematic cohomological classification of all possible mobility constraints on excitations arising from generalized symmetries — higher-form, subsystem, or higher-group — in 3+1 dimensions?

My early work on composite subsystem symmetries showed that sub-dimensional excitations can be decorated in ways that constrain their mobility. The general question of which symmetry structures produce which mobility constraints feels tractable using group cohomology, but has not been worked out in full generality. A complete classification would tell us which fracton models are genuinely distinct and which are related by gauging.

fractons subsystem symmetries generalized symmetries group cohomology
05

Are the modular data of a non-Abelian topological phase — its S and T matrices — sufficiently constrained by internal consistency conditions to bootstrap new topological orders, analogous to how crossing symmetry bootstraps CFT spectra?

The conformal bootstrap derives strong constraints on CFT data from self-consistency alone. An analogous program for modular tensor categories would use the Verlinde formula, modular covariance, and braiding non-degeneracy to constrain which anyon spectra are physically realizable. This could be a practical tool for predicting new topological phases and ruling out exotic anyon models without constructing an explicit Hamiltonian.

topological field theory modular tensor categories anyon bootstrap categorical symmetry
