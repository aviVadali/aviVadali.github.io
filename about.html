About

I grew up in Chicago and came to Caltech in 2022 to study physics. I'll be joining MIT as a PhD student in condensed matter theory in fall 2026.

Bio

My research is in theoretical condensed matter physics, with a focus on topological phases of matter. I am currently working with Jason Alicea and Pablo Sala on the stability of non-Abelian topological order to the proliferation of multiple anyon species — asking what phase transitions occur when anyons condense, and whether quantum information stored in a topological code survives the process.

I am also finishing a project with Tomo Soejima and Ashvin Vishwanath on Berry curvature in spinors subjected to periodic potentials — we find that emergent symmetries near band touchings can dramatically restructure the topology of moiré flat bands.

Before settling into condensed matter, I worked on cellular automata with Ari Turner, on subsystem symmetries and fracton topological order with Xie Chen, and on machine learning for quantum circuit verification at Fermilab. I like problems where structure appears unexpectedly from simple rules.

Outside physics, I am Vice President of the Caltech Undergraduate Physics Club. I enjoy weightlifting, reading, walking unreasonably long distances, and studying Latin, which I have been doing on and off for several years.
